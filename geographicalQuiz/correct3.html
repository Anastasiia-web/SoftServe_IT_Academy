Which of the following is the longest rail route in the world?

Trans-Siberian Rail Route is the longest rail route in the world. It was built between 1891 and 1916 to connect Moscow with the Far-East city of Vladivostok. En route it passes through the cities of Perm, Yekaterinburg, Omsk, Novosibirsk, Krasnoyarsk, Irkutsk, Chita and Khabarovsk.
Nowadays, it connects St. Petersburg in the west and Vladivostok in the east.
